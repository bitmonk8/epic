# U1-R5: orchestrator / Simplification

## Summary

The orchestrator contains significant structural duplication between `execute_leaf` and `leaf_fix_loop` (identical retry/escalation state machines), repeated `get`/`get_mut` lookup-and-unwrap boilerplate, and three repetitions of the scope-check-then-fail pattern. Extracting shared logic would reduce the file by roughly 20% and make the retry policy easier to maintain in one place.

## Notes

- The duplication between `execute_leaf` and `leaf_fix_loop` is the most impactful item. They share the same escalation state machine but operate on `task.attempts` vs `task.fix_attempts` and call `agent.execute_leaf` vs `agent.fix_leaf`. A higher-order function or trait-object closure would unify them.
- The repeated `get(id).ok_or(TaskNotFound(id))?` pattern is understandable given Rust's borrow checker constraints (cannot hold `&mut self` while also holding `&Task`), but short helper methods would still reduce visual noise without changing semantics.
- No critical issues found; the logic is correct and the complexity is inherent to the retry/recovery protocol. The suggestions target maintainability and reduction of copy-paste drift risk.
