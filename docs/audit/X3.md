# X3: Compiler warnings

## Summary
`cargo build` produces zero warnings. `cargo clippy` (with all+pedantic+nursery enabled) produces 12 pedantic/nursery warnings, all in `src/main.rs`. Across the codebase there are 17 `#[allow(...)]` suppressions; most are justified with accurate comments, but a few have misleading justifications or mask genuinely dead code.

## Notes
- The project enables clippy `all`, `pedantic`, and `nursery` lint groups in `Cargo.toml` (lines 27-29), which is stricter than most Rust projects. The 12 unsuppressed clippy warnings are all from pedantic/nursery tiers and do not indicate correctness issues.
- The `#[allow(dead_code)]` suppressions on `TuiTask` (finding 3) represent the highest risk: blanket-suppressing an entire struct can hide fields that accumulate over time without ever being used. Per-field suppression would be safer.
- No `unsafe` code was found. No `#[allow(unused_imports)]` or `#[allow(unused_variables)]` suppressions exist, which is a positive signal.
